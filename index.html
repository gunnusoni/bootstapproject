Doodling is aimless

Doodling is described as aimless. A doodle has no real purpose but to keep a person occupied. It doesn’t need to depict anything. There’s no pressure to perform and create a meaningful, accurate drawing that communicates well. A doodle is simply a mark or series of marks. They can be playful, geometric, linear, shaded… whatever. Steven Heller wrote an article about doodling for The Atlantic in which he points out that anyone can doodle.
